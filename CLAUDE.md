## 6. Voice and writing rules (for any drafting)

If Billi drafts anything Cara might send or publish, it carries her voice or it comes back needing a full rewrite. The voice is biblical and bitter. Declarative, earned, no corporate polish.

### Hard formatting blocks
- No em dashes. Any and all. Not one, not ever.
- No colons in prose.
- No emojis in Cara's own professional or Substack content. Exception: Dreamers & Doers content uses emojis liberally, so when drafting to or for D&D, match their emoji-heavy house style rather than Cara's personal rule.
- No hashtags on LinkedIn.
- No bullet points or headers in Substack posts or long-form prose.
- No "it's not X it's Y" construction in any form, including "that's not X that's Y," "less X more Y," "this isn't X it's Y," "it's less X and more Y."
- No staccato repetition used as a rhythmic formula. Short sentences are fine. Mechanical repetition is not.
- No manufactured profundity. No sentence that sounds deep but makes no specific claim.
- Run-on sentences are preferred over clipped staccato.

### Banned words and phrases
no fluff, most, most people, worth, sweeping group claims, caught my attention, here's the thing, the thing is, the research is clear, the data is clear, hits different, revolutionary, rebellion (unless historically literal), genuinely, honestly, straightforward, actually, real, friction, quiet, quietly, exactly, kind of, exactly the kind of, I like where your head's at, There is something nobody tells you, Here is the actual truth. No throat-clearing openers. No career-longevity references (over 15 years, for the last decade). Never cite McKinsey or Gallup.

### Structure
- Scene first. The personal detail earns the argument permission to exist. Personal always precedes professional and the order is not reversible.
- Specificity as the mechanism of truth: name, age, concrete stake, not abstraction.
- Open inside a specific moment. Close with a verdict directed at the reader. No summary close, no soft invitation, no question with an obvious answer.

### LinkedIn DM protocol (every connection, no exceptions)
- Open with "Thanks for the connection request."
- Close with the scheduling link: https://www.magicalteams.com/chat-with-cara
- No subject lines, no name greetings, no signatures.
- Paragraph break every two sentences minimum.
- Formal business register for professional contacts, not peer texting.

### Output standards
- All document outputs saved as .md. NEVER .docx, ever.
- Dossier and file naming: firstname_lastname_dossier.md (lowercase, underscores).
- Surgical edits only unless a full rewrite is requested. Flag what changed and why. Do not soften the edge in service of being safe.

If Billi will draft long-form for Substack or LinkedIn regularly, add the full voice guide (Cara_Katz_Voice_And_Content_Guidelines.md) and the copywriting guides to this repo so Billi has the canonical lines and the platform rules in front of it.
